# Methodology

PMO-in-a-Box is grounded in two established bodies of knowledge: the **P3M3 maturity
model** and **PMI good practice**. This page gives a short, practical overview and links
to the official sources. The frameworks themselves are **referenced for guidance only and
are not redistributed here**: please obtain the official manuals from their owners.

## P3M3®: Portfolio, Programme & Project Management Maturity Model

P3M3 is a widely-used model for assessing how mature an organization's portfolio,
programme, and project management capabilities are, and for planning improvement.

**Three sub-models**: assess at the level(s) relevant to you:
- **PfM3**: Portfolio Management
- **PgM3**: Programme Management
- **PjM3**: Project Management

**Five maturity levels**, broadly: 1) Awareness, 2) Repeatable, 3) Defined,
4) Managed, 5) Optimized.

**Seven process perspectives** assessed at each level:
1. Management Control
2. Benefits Management
3. Financial Management
4. Stakeholder Management
5. Risk Management
6. Organizational Governance
7. Resource Management

The self-assessment workbook in [`../01-assess`](../01-assess) follows this exact
structure (sub-model × level × perspective) so you can produce a baseline and a target.

**Official source:** P3M3 is owned by **Axelos / PeopleCert**: https://www.peoplecert.org
and https://www.axelos.com

## PMI good practice

The delivery templates in [`../04-operate`](../04-operate) and the design artifacts in
[`../02-design`](../02-design) reflect generally-accepted **PMI good practice**: clear
charters, business cases, defined scope, RACI accountability, and disciplined risk,
issue, and change management across the project life cycle.

**Benefits realization**: the approach in [`../02-design`](../02-design) aligns with
PMI's view that benefits should be identified, planned, and tracked through to
realization, not assumed at hand-over.

**Official source:** **Project Management Institute (PMI)**: https://www.pmi.org

## A note on maturity

Maturity is a journey, not a switch. A practical path:
1. **Baseline** honestly with the P3M3 self-assessment.
2. **Pick one or two perspectives** to improve first (often Management Control and
   Benefits Management).
3. **Embed** the relevant templates into how work actually gets done.
4. **Re-assess** periodically and raise the bar.
